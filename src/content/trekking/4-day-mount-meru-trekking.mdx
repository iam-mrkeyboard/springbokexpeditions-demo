Mount Meru is Tanzania's second-highest mountain and one of East Africa's most underrated treks. Rising to 4,566 metres inside Arusha National Park, it offers something Kilimanjaro cannot — wildlife on the trail, a dramatic volcanic crater summit, and a fraction of the crowd.

The 4-day itinerary is the recommended pace. You gain altitude steadily across four days, with two nights at Saddle Hut and Miriakamba Hut, simple but comfortable mountain huts with solar lighting and shared dining. An armed ranger walks with you on the lower slopes, where giraffe and buffalo are common sights.

Trekking Meru before Kilimanjaro is one of the best acclimatization strategies available — and it is also a spectacular standalone climb in its own right.
