There are safaris, and then there is the calving. Every January, more than 1.5 million wildebeest gather on the Ndutu Plains. By mid-month, the first calves are dropping. Within three weeks, the plains are alive with 500,000 newborns, all of them vulnerable — and the predators know it. Lions grow fat on the abundance. Cheetahs pick off stragglers. Hyena clans hunt in coordinated packs. For a wildlife photographer, this is paradise. For anyone, it's unforgettable.
