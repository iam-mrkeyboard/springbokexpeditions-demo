The cultural tours most operators offer are an hour at a Maasai village, a few photos, and back in the van. That's not what we do. We work with the communities themselves, and we pay them directly. The Hadzabe you meet are the ones who decided to share their morning with you. The Maasai who welcome you into their home are the ones who live there. It's slower, deeper, and more rewarding than any staged performance — and it puts money where it matters.
