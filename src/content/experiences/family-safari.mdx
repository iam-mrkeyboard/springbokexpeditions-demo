faqs:
  - q: What ages does this work for?
    a: We've designed family safaris for kids from 4 to 17. The trip changes a lot based on the ages - a 6-year-old's itinerary is very different from a 14-year-old's.
  - q: Are there malaria risks?
    a: Yes, in the safari areas below 1,800m. Speak to your doctor 6–8 weeks before travel. We can also route the trip around higher-altitude areas to reduce risk if you'd prefer.
  - q: Can we bring a childminder?
    a: Yes, we can arrange a local childminder or nanny for evenings at the lodges. They're usually university students studying hospitality, and they speak English.
  - q: What about vaccinations?
    a: You'll need to consult your doctor, but standard recommendations include yellow fever (if coming from an endemic country), hepatitis A and B, typhoid, and routine boosters.
