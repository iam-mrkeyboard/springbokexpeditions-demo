description: |
  The Great Migration is the largest land-animal movement on Earth — about 1.5 million wildebeest, 200,000 zebra, and 300,000 Thomson's gazelle moving in a continuous loop through the Serengeti and the Masai Mara. The most dramatic moment of the year is the Mara River crossing, when the herds plunge into the water to reach the greener grasses of the Masai Mara, and the crocodiles are waiting.

  We base you in the northern Serengeti for three to four nights during the crossing season (usually late July through October), with daily drives to the river and a guide who knows which crossing point is most likely to fire on any given day. The crossings can happen at any time of day, and they can be over in five minutes or take all afternoon — patience is the key.

faqs:
  - q: When are the river crossings?
    a: Late July to October, with the peak usually in August and September. There's no guaranteed date — your guide will know where the herds are and what's likely to happen.
  - q: Can we extend to the Masai Mara?
    a: Yes, we can extend the trip across the border into Kenya's Masai Mara. There's an extra cost for the Kenyan park fees, and you may need a multiple-entry visa.
  - q: Is this trip suitable for kids?
    a: It's fine for kids 8+. The drives can be long on the river days, and patience is required, but most children handle it well.
  - q: Why is it so expensive?
    a: The northern Serengeti camps are seasonal — they exist only during the migration. The cost of flying you in and out, the camp fees, and the peak-season demand add up.
