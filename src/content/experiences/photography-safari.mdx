  A photography safari is a different kind of trip. The game drives start earlier and finish later. The vehicle is set up for shooting - beanbags, charging points, a swivel seat at the front, and a pop-top roof that opens on three sides. The guide has been briefed on what you want to come home with, and the route is built around the light, not the itinerary.

  We work with a small group of guides who are also serious photographers, and we can pair you with one who shoots the way you do - birding, big cats, landscapes, or all of the above. The lodges we use have charging stations in the rooms and a separate secure area for camera gear.

faqs:
  - q: Can I bring a 600mm lens?
    a: Yes. The pop-top roof is designed to take long lenses on beanbags, and the vehicle has a charging port for camera batteries.
  - q: Is the guide also a photographer?
    a: All of the guides we use for photo safaris are working photographers themselves, with bodies of work they can show you. They know positioning, light, and animal behaviour.
  - q: Can I bring a drone?
    a: Drones are restricted in Tanzania's national parks. We can help you get the permits needed for some areas (Serengeti, Ngorongoro), but it's not a given - let us know at booking.
  - q: What's the best time for a photo safari?
    a: July to October for the migration and the dry-season light. January to February for the calving season in the southern Serengeti.
