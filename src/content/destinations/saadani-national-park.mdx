Saadani is the only national park in East Africa that borders the Indian Ocean, and the only place in the region where you can take a game drive through coastal woodland and end up on a white sand beach. The combination is unique, and so is the wildlife mix: the same coastal elephants that walk the forest in the morning might be seen swimming in the surf in the afternoon.

The park is small (1,100 km²) and the wildlife is less dense than the Northern Circuit, but Saadani offers a completely different experience. The vegetation is dry coastal forest and thicket, the animals are a mix of typical safari species (elephant, lion, buffalo, giraffe, zebra) and coastal specials (lesser kudu, gerenuk, the occasional hippo in the sea), and the absence of crowds gives the place a slow, quiet feel that's hard to find elsewhere.

Boat safaris on the **Wami River** are a highlight. The river is lined with mangrove and palm, and the water holds hippos, crocodiles, and a wide variety of waterbirds. The river mouth is a turtle-nesting beach, and during the November–March season, guided night walks can take you to see a green turtle laying her eggs.

Most visitors stay 2–3 nights and combine with a longer Northern Circuit safari or a few days on Zanzibar. Saadani is also a popular weekend trip from Dar es Salaam. The historic village of Saadani itself — once a Swahili trading port — is worth an hour or two, especially for the old mosque and the graves of 19th-century merchants.
