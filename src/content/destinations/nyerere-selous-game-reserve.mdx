Selous Game Reserve was renamed Nyerere National Park in 2019 in honour of Tanzania's founding president, but most travellers and operators — including us — still use the old name. With 50,000 km², the reserve is the largest protected area in Africa and one of the largest in the world. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, recognised specifically because so much of it remains genuinely wild.

The river system is the heart of the reserve. The Rufiji and its tributaries drain much of southern Tanzania, and the rivers are choked with hippos, waterbirds, and some of Africa's biggest Nile crocodiles. A boat safari along the Rufiji is one of the signature experiences of any Tanzania trip — drifting past pods of hippos while fish eagles call from the fig trees is unforgettable. The boat safaris are the easiest way to see wildlife, and they offer a perspective that no vehicle-based safari can match.

The reserve is also a stronghold for African wild dogs. Selous/Nyerere holds the largest population of wild dogs in Africa — an estimated 1,300 individuals spread across multiple breeding packs. Walking safaris led by experienced rangers offer some of the best chances anywhere in Africa to find them. The walking safaris in Nyerere are considered among the finest in the country: the open miombo woodland makes for excellent tracking, and the rivers and lakes create a constantly changing landscape.

Nyerere works well as a 3–4 night addition to a Northern Circuit safari, combined with a direct flight to the reserve. It is also a popular pairing with the coast — many travellers fly directly from Nyerere to Zanzibar for a few days of beach at the end of their trip.
