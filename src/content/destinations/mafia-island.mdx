Mafia is the island that Zanzibar was 30 years ago. The population is small (around 50,000), the roads are sandy tracks, and there's almost no development. There are only a handful of lodges on the entire island, which means the beaches are quiet, the reefs are healthy, and the only thing on the schedule is what the tide is doing.

The headline attraction is the **Mafia Island Marine Park**, which covers the southern half of the island and the surrounding reefs. The park protects one of the most important marine ecosystems in East Africa: coral gardens, seagrass meadows, and nesting beaches for green and hawksbill turtles. The marine park is also one of the most reliable places in the world to swim with **whale sharks** — they come to the area year-round to feed on plankton, and the snorkelling encounters are usually close, gentle, and unforgettable. Peak season is October to March.

Away from the marine park, Mafia has a quiet charm. **Chole Bay** has the island's best snorkelling from the beach, and dhow trips to the surrounding sandbanks are a daily ritual at the lodges. The small historical ruins of **Kua** on Juani Island — an ancient Swahili trading settlement, abandoned in the 19th century — are worth a half-day trip, both for the history and the boat journey through the mangroves.

Most visitors stay 4–5 nights and combine with 5–7 nights in the Northern Circuit (fly Dar–Mafia–Selous/Nyerere or Dar–Mafia–Zanzibar). Mafia is not a party island or a shopping destination. It's for travellers who want to dive, snorkel, sit on a quiet beach, and feel like they're somewhere that hasn't been overrun.
