Mount Meru is Tanzania's secret weapon. Most visitors arrive planning to climb Kilimanjaro and leave raving about the mountain they did first. Rising to 4,566 metres inside Arusha National Park, Meru is everything Kili is not - uncrowded, scenically varied, alive with wildlife, and crowned by one of the most dramatic summit ridges in Africa.

The single route - the **Momella Route** - begins at Momella Gate, deep inside the park. On day one you walk through montane forest with an armed park ranger at the front of the group, watching giraffe and buffalo drift through the trees. There is nothing quite like it on any other mountain on the continent. The trail climbs steadily to Miriakamba Hut, then to Saddle Hut, and at midnight on day three or four you start the final push - a narrow ridge between the outer and inner Meru crater - to reach Socialist Peak in time for sunrise.

The summit is a genuine mountain summit, not a high pass. The view stretches from Kilimanjaro's glaciers on the eastern horizon to the Rift Valley lakes below, with the sheer walls of the Meru crater dropping away on either side of the ridge. It is one of the few places in East Africa where you can watch the sun rise over the continent's highest peak while standing on the continent's fifth-highest.

Most trekkers choose between two itineraries. The **3-day climb** compresses the experience for fit, experienced hikers and skips the Little Meru acclimatisation hike. The **4-day climb** adds a night at Miriakamba Hut on the descent and a proper acclimatisation day at Saddle Hut, with the recommended side-trip up Little Meru (3,820 m) for sunset views of Kilimanjaro. The 4-day version has a summit success rate around 90%; the 3-day drops to roughly 80%.

Trekking Meru before Kilimanjaro is one of the best acclimatisation strategies available - a few days above 3,000 m on Meru dramatically increases your chances of standing on Uhuru Peak. But the mountain is also a superb standalone climb: quieter, more intimate, and far more varied than any Kilimanjaro route.
